2026 Regional Predictions

Eight regionals, one weekend — here is how Indiana's {{ year }} Girls field could shake out if every sectional finalist toed the line again.

By Track Insights · Updated {{ updated_label }}

Every May, four sectional meets feed each of Indiana's eight regionals — and the regional roster is, in essence, a remix of the sectional results from the prior week. Regional Predictions takes the {{ year }} Girls sectional results we already have in the books and asks a simple what-if: if those same finalists ran their same marks one week later, on the regional stage, who would come out on top?

It is not a forecast of who will win. Wind, weather, injury, scratch sheets and rivalry adrenaline can swing a score by twenty points. But as a baseline — a measuring stick that uses real, recorded performances from this season — it is the closest thing we have to a side-by-side.

The Method

How the math works

The model is intentionally plain. For each of the eight regionals, it grabs the final-round results from the four sectionals that feed it — Regional 1 from Sectionals 1–4, Regional 2 from 5–8, and so on. Inside each event it re-ranks every athlete by their sectional mark (fastest time wins for track events; best mark wins for field events), then hands out points the way Indiana scores at regionals:

10 · 8 · 6 · 5 · 4 · 3 · 2 · 1

Ties split the points evenly. Relays score the same way as individual events. Add it all up across thirteen individual events and three relays, and the team totals fall out. Note: in events where athletes run a preliminary heat to qualify for the final (100m, 200m, and sprint hurdles), only the final-round marks are used for scoring.

Methodology

The fine print

  • Each regional is fed by the four sectionals numbered (N−1)·4+1 through (N−1)·4+4.
  • Only final-round individual results are used; prelim times are excluded.
  • Relays are scored on the same 10-8-6-5-4-3-2-1 scale as individual events.
  • Ties at a placement average the points that would have been awarded.
  • Results with placeholder marks (0 or 9999) are filtered out.
  • This is a paper projection from sectional data — it does not account for scratches, weather, or athletes who chose not to enter an event.
